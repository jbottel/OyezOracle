FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Where does the name OyezOracle come from?

At the beginning of each session of oral arguments, the Supreme Court comes into session with a traditional statement by the court marshal:

"The Honorable, the Chief Justice and the Associate Justices of the Supreme Court of the United States. Oyez! Oyez! Oyez! All persons having business before the Honorable, the Supreme Court of the United States, are admonished to draw near and give their attention, for the Court is now sitting. God save the United States and this Honorable Court.

Oyez, pronounced Oh-YAY, is an interjection that has been used in courts in the US and UK for hundreds of years. It means roughly "hear ye." OyezOracle attempts to predict the future actions of the Supreme Court and is therefore the Oyez Oracle.

Does OyezOracle use liberal/conservative leanings of the Justices to make predictions?

Yes, this is the one piece of information it uses that is not necessarily found within the oral argument transcripts. OyezOracle uses a Martin-Quinn score to represent the justice's idealogical leanings and this is described in the Identifying Features section.

How far back does OyezOracle's training go?

Training for the entire history of the court would be an extremely difficult process, and ultimately the justices do change over time, so OyezOracle focuses on the most recent iteration of the court, starting in the year 2010 with the introduction of the most junior justice, Elena Kagan.

How long is an oral argument?

Typically an argument is one hour long, and the court hears two of these arguments in a single day.Transcripts are usually available within a single day.

Why does it take so long to make a prediction?

First, OyezOracle parses all of the transcript sections and identifies who the speakers are. The longest part of the process is identifying the different parts of speech that a justice uses in their speech. After this, the quickest part, plugging of the scores into the network occurs.
