A reasonable assumption to make based on a simple examination of the data is that a prediction rarely strays much from 0.5. This makes sense, since most cases are not easy to predict and often are decided by a single vote. It is possible that this could be improved with the itnroduction of justice-centered networks, as described below.

Accuracy in Prediction

The available cases from 2010-2015 were placed into two sets: training and verification. 75% percent of the cases went to training the network, and 25% were used to verify. The network correctly predicted 63.3% of the cases in the verification set.

Future Work to Improve Accuracy

OyezOracle currently uses a single network to predict the entire supreme court. The upside of this approach is that some justices decisions are heavily correlated, and a silent justice like Clarence Thomas can usually be expected to vote along with a justice like Antonin Scalia. The downside of this approach is that the justices are each very different, and looking at the court as a whole might not pick up on these difference like an individual network for each justice would.

A future approach for OyezOracle involves a network for each justice, and deriving the result from that, which will hopefully lead to higher levels of accuracy.
