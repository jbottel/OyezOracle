Supreme Court Arguments

The U.S. Supreme Court, like many courts, builds upon many years of tradition and is ordered around formal protocol. Every case that comes before the U.S. Supreme Court follows a very exact series of events. If a case is not otherwise dismissed beforehand, every case that comes before the court will have an oral argument. During this argument, adovcates for both the petitioner and respondent will present a final oral argument in front of the nine justices. During this argument, the advocate is usually subjected to some level of questioning by the justices.

Aside from these oral arguments, we hear nothing else from the justices on their perceptions. In fact, it has been said that the justices don't even know how their colleageues feel about a certain case until the oral argument begins. Given that this is the only public feedback that the justices will give on an individual case, it is the perfect place to go to find the information required to make a prediction.
